About Tensor


Tensor is an annual 17-hour Datathon organized by Techwarts_Predict_This, Developer Student Club (DSC) and ACM PESU-ECC Student Chapter. It is the first iteration of its kind that will be held in PES University EC Campus on 14th to 15th March from 5pm to 12pm.

A Datathon is a competition where you are challenged to work on a real-world business case from different areas of Machine Learning, AI, and Data Science. All the selected teams will be provided with a dataset and respective problem statements to be solved.The teams based on their performance will be filtered in various rounds. The participants have the freedom to work on the language and tools of their choice for cleaning, visualising and building the Machine Learning model.

Code of Conduct

  1. All the participants are requested to be present at the stipulated start time of the event for registration.
  2. Participants must bring the NOC( No Objection Certificate), which has to be submitted by each participant in the beginning of the event, duly signed.
  3. Carrying the event ID card/pass at all times is compulsory, as this will help us identify the participants of the event and it is also required to avail snacks and dinner.
  4. All the team members are required to carry their respective college ID cards.
  5. No participant will be allowed to leave the campus premises at any point of time. If any participant wants to leave, he/she would be required to submit a letter of request, and can then only leave after a written approval.
  6. If any team has any problem, they must approach any of the organising committee members, who will be identifiable by their T-shirts.
  7. PES University is a prestigious institution with strict rules against smoking, consumption of alcohol and drugs. If anybody is found violating these rules, strict action will be taken against them.
  8. All participants are required to abide by these rules and regulations.

What is a Datathon?

A Datathon is a competition where you are challenged to work on a real-world business case from different domains based on the provided datasets

Participants have the freedom to work on the language and tools of their choice for cleaning, visualising and building the Machine Learning model

How will the judging process work?

There will be a panel of judges and mentors who will review the datasets in three rounds of judging

There are two categories : General and Beginner. All the first years will be placed under the Beginner category.


Round 1 @ 12AM

In this round. The panel of Judges will review the datasets and suggest modifications.
No scoring will be done during this round

Round 2 @ 10AM

In this round. Two judges will be assigned to each team and the evalutation will be done by both the judges individually. Based on the final scores, 10 finalists from the General category and 5 finalists from the Beginner category will be shortlisted to compete in the final round

Round 3 @ 12PM

This is the Final round. Each of the finalists have to present their solution on stage to the panel of judges



Scoring will be based on:

Ingenuity

Improvements to existing solutions, additional data sources used. Applicable on both data processing done, and model building.

Techniques used

Machine learning methods used, such as regression, clustering, reinforcement learning etc. Multiple methods or advanced methods will be granted more points. Along with these techniques, participants have to explain why they chose that model, and how it helps them.

Accuracy

70-80% = 2 points
80-90% = 3 points
90-95% = 4 points
95%+ = 5 points

Scalability

Can the model solve a real-world problem effectively, or does it require major modifications for it to work in the real world? Can it handle issues that come along with scaling?


Total for each attribute is 5 points
